# Touring Entertainment Production — Market & Cost Research
*Compiled: 2026-03-25 | Sources: 50+*

## KEY NUMBERS FOR MANIFEST

### Market Size
- Global live entertainment: **$202.9B (2025)** → $270B by 2030
- US live music alone: **$18.5B (2025)**
- Global exhibition market: **$57.2B (2025)** → $88.7B by 2035
- Corporate events: **$330.9B (2023)** → $730.7B by 2035
- Top 100 touring artists: **$8.9B grossed (2025)**
- ~30% of touring revenue = production/logistics → **~$2.7B just for top 100 tours**
- 47,753 trade show/conference businesses in the US alone

### Production Cost Breakdown
- Staging/production: **20-30% of gross revenue**
- Transportation/freight: **10-13% of total budget**
- Labor (install crew): **18-25% of total budget**
- Equipment rentals: **10-15% of total budget**
- Venue/drayage fees: **35% of total budget** (for exhibitors)

### Trucking Costs
- Operating cost per mile: **$2.26** (ATRI 2024)
- Market rate per mile: **$2.15-$4.65** (2026)
- 53' trailer rental: **$550-$800/month** or **$45-$65/day** for chassis
- Entertainment trailers: **$1,500-$2,500+/day**
- Event hauling driver pay: **$390/day** + fuel surcharge
- Major tour truck counts: 30-120 trucks (Taylor Swift: 90, U2: 120, Beyonce: 62)
- First-tier act: ~30 trucks, up to **$750,000/day** operating cost

### Labor Rates
- IATSE stagehand average: **$37.43/hr** (Local 15), national avg **$21.92/hr**
- Trade show I&D labor: **$90-$220/hr** per person (union)
- Benefits add-on: **$24-$36+/hr** on top
- Overtime: **1.5x** after 8 hrs, **2x** after 12 hrs, **3x** after 15 hrs (low budget)
- Non-union crew: **$150-$400/day**
- Broadway tour: ~50-60 people moving, ~$300K/week operating

### Equipment Rentals
- Scissor lifts: **$100-$425/day**
- Boom lifts: **$250-$1,500/day**
- Forklifts: **$200-$700/day** + operator $30-$60/hr
- Chain motors: **$125-$160/day**
- LED video wall: **$40-$70/sq ft/day**
- Arena sound rig: **~$15,000/night**

### Rush Order Markups
- Standard expedited freight: **20-30%** over standard
- Rush fee / immediate dispatch: **20-50%**
- Team drivers (long distance): **40-60%** premium
- Weekend/Holiday service: **30-100%** premium
- Rush production (fabricated goods): **25-50%**, up to 100%
- Bulk vs single unit shipping: **$0.20/unit vs $10/unit** (50x difference)
- Bulk purchasing saves: **up to 27% on average**

### Waste & Inefficiency
- **25-40%** of trade show investment wasted (missteps, poor planning)
- **10-20% premium** on show services ordered last minute
- **53%** of event planners say increasing costs are greatest challenge
- **80%** of trade show problems are avoidable
- Drayage costs increased **257%** between 1996-2013
- Concert costs up **75%** in last 10 years
- Structured budget systems reduce overruns by **85%**

### Competitor Landscape
- **No direct competitor** doing what MANIFEST does (production cost optimization + analysis)
- Existing tools: Master Tour (80K users, tour management), Prism.fm (venue booking), Cvent ($4.6B acquisition)
- General contractors: Freeman ($1-5B), GES (~$879M), Shepard ($107M)
- Tour trucking: Special Event Transportation (100+ trucks), Upstaging, PRG (43 locations, 5 continents)
- **GAP: Nobody is doing cost ANALYSIS and OPTIMIZATION** — they're doing execution/logistics

### Industry Pain Points
- "Spreadsheets don't scale — they break under pressure"
- Coordinating across multiple venues is #1 challenge
- Version control nightmares across fragmented systems
- "Every manual entry is another opportunity for something to slip"
- Rising costs across insurance, labor, equipment
- Last-minute changes create chain reaction failures
- Don't know real cost until invoices arrive months later
