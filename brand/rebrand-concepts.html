Brand Exploration

Five name directions. Each designed to communicate what the company actually does — find waste and cut costs for touring production companies. No tech startup energy. Think consulting firm meets production industry insider.

Concept 01 — Manifest

A manifest is a shipping document — every truck has one. "To manifest" means to make visible. You make waste visible. Double meaning, industry-native.

Dark
Light
Make waste visible. Every dollar, accounted for. See where the money goes.

Why it works: Every production manager knows what a manifest is. It's industry language. And the verb meaning — "to make apparent" — is literally your value prop. It sounds established, not startup-y. "Manifest Production Intelligence" as a descriptor tells them exactly what you do.

Concept 02 — Delta

The mathematical symbol for difference. You sell the gap between what they spend and what they should spend. Simple. Powerful. Universal.

Dark
Light
Find the gap. Close the gap. The difference is in the details. From what you spend to what you should.

Why it works: Delta (Δ) is universally understood as "the difference." Your entire business is about showing people the delta between their actual spend and optimal spend. It's short, sharp, mathematical, and sounds like a serious firm. "Delta Production Cost Group" sounds like it's been around for 20 years. Caution: Delta is common — Delta Airlines, Delta Force. You'd need the descriptor to differentiate.

Concept 03 — Meridian

A line of longitude — the invisible line that brings precision to navigation. You bring precision to production budgets. Global connotation, premium sound.

Dark
Light
Precision for productions. Navigate your costs. The line between waste and profit.

Why it works: Meridian sounds like an established consulting firm. It suggests global reach (you're in Barcelona, working worldwide), precision, and navigation. "Meridian Production Partners" sounds like a $50M company. The globe icon reinforces the touring/global nature of the business.

Concept 04 — Callout

In production, a "callout" is when you flag something that needs attention. That's literally what you do — call out the waste. Direct. Memorable. Action-oriented.

Dark
Light
We call it out. You cut it out. Flag the waste. Fix the margin. Someone had to say it.

Why it works: It's confrontational in a good way. It says "we're not here to be polite, we're here to tell you what's wrong and fix it." The exclamation mark icon is bold and unmistakable at any size. "Callout Production Cost Advisors" positions you as straight-talking experts, not consultants who bill by the hour and say nothing.

Concept 05 — AUROS (Evolved)

Keep the name but reposition it. Drop the "AI" completely. Add a descriptor that grounds it in the industry. New mark, same equity.

Dark
Light
See what you're missing. Precision for productions. Gold standard. No waste.

Why it works: You've already built brand equity with AUROS — domain, Cal.com, landing page. Rebranding has a cost. By adding "Production Cost Intelligence" as a descriptor and dropping any mention of "AI," you reposition without losing what you've built. The geometric A mark with precision ticks adds gravitas. "Auros" still means gold (from Latin "aurum") — and gold is what you save them.

My recommendation

Strongest option: MANIFEST. It's industry-native language, it has a powerful double meaning (the document + making visible), it doesn't sound like a tech company, and "Manifest Production Intelligence" tells someone exactly what you do in three words. The clipboard/checklist icon scales well and feels operational, not decorative.

Safest option: AUROS evolved. You keep the domain, the Cal.com, and everything already built. Just add the descriptor and the new mark. Lower risk, faster to execute.

Boldest option: CALLOUT. If you want to be remembered. It's polarizing in the best way — "we call out waste." But it might feel too aggressive for some enterprise buyers.
